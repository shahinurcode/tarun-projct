Mission & Philosophy

Across the developing world, new technologies are improving the distribution of resources for education, connectivity, health and more. However, the multinational companies who are creating these technologies are structured to operate in economies with well-developed institutional infrastructures and are not equipped to navigate the various challenges of the developing world.

Since 2000, consumer spending in Sub-Saharan Africa has grown at a steady four percent per year, reaching nearly $600 billion in 2010. The market is expected to be worth $1 trillion by 2020. However expansive these numbers may be, the purchasing power of Africans on average is still significantly lower than that of people in the developed world.

In order for multinational firms to properly cater to this market, they must offer a tailored business model that offers beneficial, but cost-justifiable products.

We enable “in-country” distributors more access to viable products at affordable pricing to resell to a large segment of the population that likely could not afford them. These products add immense economic benefit for the producers and local distributors, as well as increase economic opportunity for end consumers in the developing nation.

The Network Generation provides a turnkey solution that

(1) Identifies & sources products that are viable in the developing world

(2) Manages quality control and delivers a final product that the in-country distributor can resell to the consumer

(3) Supplies value added products to local distributors at a cost that enables the dealer to make a profit and meet the growing demand for products to the end consumer at a purchase price they can justify.
